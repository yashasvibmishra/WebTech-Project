The Monetary Aspects of our Extension Suggestions
No cost/low cost measures
Medium and acceptable cost measures
High cost measures
No cost/low cost measures
Switching of lights, idle running equipment, insulation of chilled or electrically heated
process fluid, better control of process variables and process equipment fall under this
category. These measures often require little or no investment, and would obviously be
the first parameters that our solution would target as this is the most easy to tackle. Both
in terms of effort and money.
Eg:
● TURNING OFF THE LIGHTS WHEN NOT IN USE
● TURNING OFF ELECTRIC STOVES SEVERAL MINUTES BEFORE THE SPECIFIED
COOKING TIME
● TURNING OFF T.V, COMPUTER AND AUDIO SYSTEMS WHEN NOT IN USE
● NOT OPENING THE DOORS OF THE REFRIGERATORS FREQUENTLY
Medium cost measures
The financial returns from no cost/low cost and medium cost measures can be used to invest in higher
cost energy conservation measures if justified. These will not cost much to the stake holders, but shall
help in reducing carbon footprints by manifolds!
Eg: Ensuring the meter connection is correct, and other such inspection of sensitive appliances
High cost measures
involving replacement of equipment with more efficient models fall in this category. Such
equipment replacement may be delayed until new purchases are required anyway, at which
time the net additional cost for higher efficiency can be easily justified. Our solutions will keep such
measures at a really low priority, as to not cause much financial headaches to our Eco-warriors!
Eg: PURCHASE ENERGY-EFFICIENT PRODUCTS
